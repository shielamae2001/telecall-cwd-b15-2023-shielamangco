Popular Places

El Nido
Location: Palawan, Philippines

El Nido is known for white-sand beaches, coral reefs and as the gateway to the Bacuit archipelago, a group of islands with steep karst cliffs. Palawan Island Philippines is the largest island. The island is half desert, so still undeveloped.

Siargao Road
Location: Siargao, Philippines

Siargao's a country's famous tourist destination, well-known for its many surfing spots and featured in the film 'Siargao' for such qualities. Siargao’s focal point for visitors is General Luna (GL), while the island’s main town, Dapa Town, has grocery shops, a pharmacy, restaurants and cafés.
Read More Here

Puerto Galera
Location: Oriental Mindoro, Philippines

Puerto Galera is popular among all nature and beach lovers. Other reason is that its long curve of White Beach is backed by bars and resorts. Inland, a forest trail leads to secluded Talipanan Falls, and a hillside 9-hole golf course sits above the coast.
Read More Here

Banaue Rice Terraces
Location: Ifugao , Philippines

The Banaue Rice Terraces are a National Cultural Treasure of the Philippines and are considered to be the 8th Natural Wonder of the World by many Filipinos. The terraces are carved in the mountains of Ifugao and were most likely done by hand.
Read More Here

Boracay Island
Location: AKlan, Philippines

Boracay Island's White Beach is the most famous beach in the Philippines because of its its crystal blue water, its pure white sand, and its magical sunsets. For more relaxing interludes, award-winning beaches beckon, as do island-hopping trips and cultural pursuits ranging from beach meditation sessions and yoga to local cookery lessons.
Read More Here

Taal Volcano
Location: Batangas, Philippines

Taal Volcano is known to be the second most active volcano in the Philippines located in the province of Batangas. Taal Volcano is a large caldera in Batangas that was formed by prehistoric eruptions. In the wake of the eruptions, it was eventually filled by Taal Lake.
